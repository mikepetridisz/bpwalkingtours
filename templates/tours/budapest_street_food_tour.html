Overview

Street food is living its golden age in Budapest. If you are curious to find out how well street food culture mixes with Hungarian flavors, this is the right tour for you. We will help you navigate the variety of dishes that can be enjoyed at food trucks and small vendors throughout the city, making sure you can try the best bites!
With us, you will not only spend a perfect day eating your way through the streets of Budapest, but you'll also learn clever bits about Hungarian history, traditions, and culture along the way. We would like to show you the places that tourists don’t always find by themselves. Take a step off the beaten track away from tourist traps, to local eateries and street vendors to experience the real Budapest. There is surely something for everyone!
Let’s start with every Hungarian’s favorite comfort food - a must in Hungary at the poolside or the beach – Lángos! The “Hungarian Pizza” is a deep-fried piece of dough, traditionally topped with sour cream, grated cheese, and garlic. If you feel more adventurous, you can try one of the many - less traditional, but nonetheless delicious – other flavors as well.
We will then take a trip back in time to the ’70s with the help of the old school local butcher shop, owned by the most famous butcher of Budapest, Uncle Joe.
Jewish influences have brought special delicacies to Hungarian cuisine - such as the “Queen of Cakes”, Flódni. This dessert, similar to a strudel with special pastry, is filled with walnut and poppy seed cream, along with grated apple, and plum jam - it is symbolic of the diversity of Hungarian cuisine (and the harmony of flavors in it).
At the end of the tour, we will grab an authentic Hungarian craft beer in one of the coolest pubs in all of Hungary. You might have thought that Hungary is all about wine, but we will be there to prove you wrong!
